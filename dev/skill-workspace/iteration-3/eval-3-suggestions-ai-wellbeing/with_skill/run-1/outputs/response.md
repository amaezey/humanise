**Suggestions, one per flag**

Generic staccato emphasis
  - "Everything becomes harder to metabolise. Small decisions feel punitive. Social contact becomes more effortful."
  Try: Combine one or two of the beats so the rhythm feels less manufactured: "Everything becomes harder to metabolise: small decisions feel punitive, and social contact takes more effort than it should."

Mechanical repeated sentence starts
  - "Sometimes it is..."
  Try: Keep one "Sometimes", then vary the rest: "It may be a clean, brutal wakefulness at 3 am. Or the mind may replay the last conversation, the hospital room, the phone call, the thing said badly, the thing never said. Sleep may come easily and break apart before dawn. The body may sleep for ten hours and still wake exhausted."

Curly quotes
  - "“good sleep habits”"
  Try: Use straight quotes if this is meant to be plain markdown: `"good sleep habits"`.

Headings in prose
  - "# The hour grief comes looking"
  Try: If this is an essay title, remove the markdown marker: `The hour grief comes looking`. If it is meant to flow as prose, cut the title and start with the opening sentence.

Dense negation
  - "But exhaustion is not devotion. Staying awake does not keep the person closer."
  Try: Turn some negatives into direct claims: "Exhaustion is a strain on the body, not a proof of love. Rest can be part of surviving the loss."

Triad density
  - "the dead feel closer, the mind gets louder, and the body refuses..."
  Try: Break some three-part lists into less symmetrical prose: "At night the dead can feel closer. The mind gets louder, and the body may refuse the simple mercy of switching off."
