Manufactured insight framing (1 instance)
  - "yes, that is the real question"
  Why: The phrase "the real question" performs hidden depth — telling the reader something significant has been uncovered without earning it through the surrounding evidence. It's a stock cue that AI prose uses to pre-package profundity.

Generic staccato emphasis
  Where: A run of 8 consecutive short sentences in the "behavioural data" passage — "Who speaks first? Who keeps returning to risk? Who reframes everything as a technology issue?…" — and a similar short-sentence cluster in the "Not because they are fun. Not because they produce sticky notes.   Why: Repeated short dramatic beats land as machine-paced emphasis rather than naturally varied rhythm. The cadence is regular enough that it reads as designed for effect, not thought.

Mechanical repeated sentence starts (anaphora)
  Where: 8 consecutive sentences opening with "Who" in the same "behavioural data" passage. The same shape recurs in slightly shorter runs ("Sometimes what changes…", "It can clarify…", "Not because…", "We'll solve…").
  Why: Templated anaphora signals scaffolded rhythm rather than intentional rhetorical use. One anaphoric run can be powerful; back-to-back runs across a piece read as default machine cadence.

Curly quotes (64 instances)

  - "current-state journey map"
  - "comms problem"
  Why: Every quotation mark in the draft is a smart/curly variant. That's typical of model output and word-processor autocorrect; plain manuscript prose is usually mixed or straight. Easy fix, high signal.

Dense negation (45 markers, 12.0 per 1000 words)
  Where: Threaded through the whole piece, but concentrated in the "Not because they are fun. Not because they produce sticky notes. Not because everyone gets to feel included…" opener; the "It is not a lesser outcome. It is not a free prelude…" assertion stack; and the closing "Not as a warm-up. Not as a free diagnostic. Not as a cheerful day of sticky notes."
  Why: Negation as a structural device — defining the thing by what it isn't — is a known AI tic. At this density it stops feeling like rhetorical sharpening and starts feeling like a reflex.

Vocabulary diversity (type-token ratio 0.260, target >0.40)
  Where: Across the full 3,685-word piece, only 959 unique words. Watch the recurrence of "clarity", "client", "problem", "workshop", "decision", "consulting", "work", "next" — they cycle through every section without lexical refresh.
  Why: Long human prose typically introduces fresh vocabulary as it explores subtopics. Heavy repetition of a small core vocabulary makes a piece feel mechanically produced even when the sentences are individually well-formed.

Triad density (25 triads)

  - "synthesis, judgement, a usable artefact"

  Why: Three-part lists are a classic AI rhythm — they sound balanced and considered. At 25 instances in one essay they stop being rhetorical and start being a default cadence the reader can hear coming.
