Generic staccato emphasis (1 instance)
- "Not disappears. Not resolves neatly. But changes."
Why: A sequence of three very short sentences used as a closing landing beat. AI defaults to this shape at the end of paragraphs to manufacture emphasis; in a piece this measured it sticks out as engineered rather than felt.

Mechanical repeated sentence starts (4 consecutive sentences with "Sometimes")
- "Sometimes it is a clean, brutal wakefulness at 3 am."
- "Sometimes it is the inability to fall asleep because the mind keeps replaying the last conversation, the hospital room, the phone call, the thing said badly, the thing never said."
- "Sometimes sleep comes easily but breaks apart before dawn."
- "Sometimes the body sleeps for ten hours and still wakes exhausted."
Why: Anaphora is a real rhetorical device, but four parallel "Sometimes…" clauses in a row reads as templated cadence. Human writers usually break the pattern by the third repetition.

Curly quotes (9 instances)

Why: Curly quotes are typography rather than proof of AI, but ChatGPT inserts them by default and they are a common giveaway in plain-text drafts that should use straight quotes.

Headings in prose

**Suggestions, one per flag**

Generic staccato emphasis
- "Not disappears. Not resolves neatly. But changes."
Try: fold the three fragments into one sentence so the beat lands without the engineered cadence — e.g. "Sleep can become one of the ways grief changes shape, not by disappearing or resolving, but by changing."

Mechanical repeated sentence starts
Try: break the parallel after the second "Sometimes". Keep the first two for rhythm, then recast the rest. e.g. "Sometimes it is a clean, brutal wakefulness at 3 am. Sometimes the mind refuses to settle, replaying the last conversation, the hospital room, the phone call. Other nights, sleep comes easily and breaks apart before dawn. Or the body sleeps for ten hours and still wakes exhausted." (The substitution itself matters less than the variation — a writer's hand stops the pattern; AI lets it run.)

Curly quotes
- "good sleep habits", "How do I force myself to sleep?", "How do I make the night less hostile?", "Now I am grieving and failing at sleep."
Try: replace every `"` and `"` with `"`, and every `'` and `'` with `'`. In most editors a find-and-replace pass takes seconds. Preserve curly quotes only if the piece is destined for typeset publication where the typesetter expects them.

Headings in prose
- "# The hour grief comes looking"
Try: drop the markdown `#` and run the title as a plain bold line, or remove the heading entirely if this is going somewhere (Substack, a magazine CMS) that adds the title separately. If the title earns a heading, write it without the markdown syntax: `The hour grief comes looking`.

Dense negation
Try: recast about half the negations as positive claims. The strongest negations to keep are the ones doing real conceptual work ("grief is not poor time management"; "exhaustion is not devotion"). Convert the others. Examples:
- "sleep is not just a pause from consciousness. It is an active biological process…" → "Sleep is an active biological process…"
- "insomnia is not always dramatic" → "Insomnia after a death often arrives quietly."
- "The goal is not to win against grief. It is to reduce the secondary panic" → "The goal is to reduce the secondary panic that says, 'Now I am grieving and failing at sleep.'"
- "exhaustion is not devotion. Staying awake does not keep the person closer." → "Exhaustion is not devotion; the body in distress is not closer to the person who died."
The aim is to break the "X is not Y, it is Z" engine so the few remaining negations carry more weight.

Triad density
Try: cut at least three of the seven triads down to a pair, a single concrete image, or a longer specific phrase. The triad to keep is probably "a face, a phrase, a scene, a tenderness" because it is doing real evocative work; the triad to cut first is "less caffeine, a dark room and no phone in bed" — these are sleep-hygiene clichés the piece is already arguing against, so listing them as a triad reinforces the very rhythm the essay critiques. Replace with: "A fixed bedtime, less caffeine, the usual sleep-hygiene script — most adults do need around 7 to 9 hours, and routine helps." Then for the others, prefer a pair ("too bright and too fast") or expand one item with detail and drop the other two.
