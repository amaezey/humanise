## Summary
Practitioner account of trying to remove AI signatures from generated text and finding em dashes persistent across prompts and settings. Csutoras argues the mark is embedded because em dashes appear widely in human training data, but also says em dashes are not a reliable standalone giveaway.

## Main insights
- Useful as mechanism/anecdotal source for em-dash persistence, not statistical evidence.
- The source itself says the em dash is not a real/reliable giveaway and points to word frequency and lack of typos as stronger clues.
- Updates matter: the piece later notes a Claude workflow for replacing em dashes and says official vendor fixes were still missing as of August 21, 2025.

## Evidence and claims to extract
- Claims: hard-coded instructions, reminders, warnings, and "critical error" labels did not reliably remove em dashes in the author's experiments.
- Mechanism claim: em dashes were common in human-written training data and not flagged as special during training.
- Caveat: the article explicitly says em dashes are not reliable AI evidence.
