## Summary
Vendor/practitioner guide listing nine signs of AI-written text: uniform sentence length, excessive hedging, em-dash overuse, generic examples, overly balanced perspectives, transition overload, perfect grammar/zero personality, repetitive paragraph structure, and lack of specific citations.

## Main insights
- Mostly restates patterns already present in stronger sources.
- The article includes useful caveats: look for multiple signals, not one; human writers can naturally exhibit some patterns; non-native/formal academic writers may show some cues.
- Treat numeric claims like "em dashes in more than 30% of sentences" as unvalidated unless corroborated.

## Evidence and claims to extract
