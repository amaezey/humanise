## Summary
Practitioner article testing whether em dashes are useful clues for AI writing. Bailey prompts six AI systems with the same article prompt and reports their em-dash counts, then argues em dashes can be a clue but cannot prove AI authorship.

## Main insights
- Useful model-specific punctuation context: ChatGPT, Copilot, and DeepSeek used many em dashes in the small test; Gemini and Meta.ai used none.
- The article explicitly warns that humans use em dashes and that the signal is easy to circumvent.
- Bailey says factual mistakes in the generated articles were more telling than punctuation.

## Evidence and claims to extract
- Test prompt: write an article about the H3H3 lawsuit against reaction streamers.
- Reported counts: ChatGPT 573 words/8 em dashes; Copilot 466/8; DeepSeek 555/9 plus 4 en dashes; Claude 948/2 plus 1 en dash; Gemini 499/0; Meta.ai 261/0.
- Stated limitations: sample too small for statistical significance; not all AI-generated works contain em dashes; humans also use them; users can replace them.
