## Summary
Data-journalism analysis of 328,744 publicly shared English-language ChatGPT messages from the GPT-4o model, May 2024 through July 2025. The article identifies style clues such as emojis, em dashes, cliches, and vocabulary drift while warning that these patterns are not exclusive to ChatGPT.

## Main insights
- Stronger than anecdotal em-dash sources because it uses a large corpus of shared ChatGPT messages.
- The reported signals are model/time-specific: GPT-4o, May 2024-July 2025.
- The article supports signal stacking and drift, not single-feature detection.

## Evidence and claims to extract
- Corpus: 328,744 publicly shared English-language ChatGPT messages, GPT-4o, May 2024-July 2025.
- Reported features from excerpts: emoji use rose, with 70% of messages by July 2025 containing at least one emoji; overused emojis included check mark, brain, and blue diamond; frequent em dashes; cliche "not just X, but Y"; "delve" declining; "core" and "modern" rising; increased contractions.
- Caveat in excerpt: patterns are not exclusive to ChatGPT and can occur in human writing.
