## Summary
Practitioner education guide listing signs teachers look for in suspected ChatGPT use: uniform writing style, overly perfect grammar/structure, repetitive or generic phrasing, superficial analysis, vocabulary mismatched to the student's level, formulaic transitions, lack of idioms/slang/cultural references, hallucinated facts, weak citations, and sudden tone/complexity shifts.

## Main insights
- Useful as classroom-facing practitioner taxonomy, but weaker than Waltzer and Murray/Tersigni for evidence.
- Some signs match stronger sources: polished grammar with weak argument/evidence, genericity, citation weakness, and student-level mismatch.
- The source should not be used to justify punitive detection; it is not empirical validation.

## Evidence and claims to extract

## Skill-use audit
- **Good use:** Generate student-writing review prompts and examples after stronger evidence is in place.
