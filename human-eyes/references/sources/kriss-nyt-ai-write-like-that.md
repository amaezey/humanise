## Summary
Style-critical magazine essay arguing that contemporary AI prose is not merely clean or neutral, but weirdly overwrought, patterned, and contagious. Kriss treats AI style as a recognizable public register: spectral/ghostly diction, quiet/whisper/hum motifs, tapestry/weaving metaphors, "not X but Y" contrast, em-dash dependence, tricolons, forced synesthesia, generic profundity, and heightened AI vocabulary.

## Main insights
- Strong direct support for literary/critical "AI voice" examples.
- The article also warns about false positives: em dashes, "delve," and elevated registers can be human, regional, or professional style rather than proof of AI.
- Kriss separates model tics from human uptake: people may begin copying AI-style phrases even without using AI.
- The strongest skill value is field-guide disambiguation, not detector certainty.
