## Summary
Aranya's Poetly post is a reflective essay on AI, poetry, young poets, and writing process, framed through Campbell McGrath's revision practice and the author's own experience of moving poems through notebooks, screens, apps, and submission contexts. The post is useful as a poetry-process source, but it does not cleanly validate the earlier repo-inherited mapping to specific AI-poetry tells like aphorism density, negate-and-redefine structure, or mood-word accumulation.

## Main insights
- The post treats poetry as a process of revision across media, interfaces, pauses, mistakes, and fresh returns to older fragments.
- It argues that the response to AI should be attuned to process rather than only output.
- It recommends comparing AI-generated poems against a poet's own archive and response, which is closer to a human-process check than a surface detector.
- It frames "mistakes," randomness, and testimony as meaningful human-poetry dimensions that AI has not convincingly reproduced.
- Walsh et al. remains the stronger directly reviewed poetry evidence.
- The source weakly supports genre-specific poetry review prompts, but not hard-coded poetry-tell rules.
