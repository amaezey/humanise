## Evidence and claims to extract
- Direct source reviewed: Neil Clarke's post content via WordPress JSON endpoint.
- Clarke reports a pandemic-era increase in spammy submissions, initially plagiarism and paraphraser output, followed by a sharp late-2022/early-2023 spike after AI chatbots gained attention.
- The post's graph alt text reports monthly banned/spam cases moving from typically below 20 to 25 in November, 50 in December, over 100 in January, and nearly 350 by mid-February 2023.
- Clarke explicitly declines to detail the patterns used to identify AI spam because publishing them would help evasion and could harm legitimate authors.
- He warns third-party detectors have false negatives and false positives, and worries that countermeasures could add barriers for new and international authors.
- The 2023-02-20 update says submissions spiked over 50 before noon and submissions were temporarily closed.
